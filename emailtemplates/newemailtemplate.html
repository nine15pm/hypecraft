Hamilton's Title Hopes in Jeopardy Amid Worst Season Start in F1 Career (ML score: 85) (Trend score: -1) (Past repeat: False)

Lewis Hamilton's final season with Mercedes has not gone as planned, with the seven-time world champion currently eighth in the drivers' championship behind teammate George Russell. Hamilton has failed to finish in the top five in a race and hasn't won since the Saudi Arabian Grand Prix in December 2021. He has only outqualified Russell once and beaten him once when both drivers have seen the chequered flag. Hamilton has expressed frustration with his one-lap pace, saying "I don't understand... I'm just lacking" and "I don't anticipate being ahead of George in Qualifying this year." Team principal Toto Wolff has acknowledged Hamilton's frustration, saying "All drivers are a bit sceptical at times" and that the team is "trying to maximise the results for Hamilton's final season." With 16 events remaining, there is still time for Hamilton to turn his season around.

F1 Drivers Demand Rule Change to Restore Strategic Excitement to Racing (ML score: 65) (Trend score: 603) (Past repeat: False)

Formula 1 drivers, including Max Verstappen and Fernando Alonso, are calling for a change to the red-flag tyre rule, which they feel eliminated strategic excitement at the Monaco Grand Prix. The rule allows drivers to change tyres under a red flag, making mandatory pitstops and strategic plays unnecessary. Verstappen and Alonso suggest alternatives, such as not changing tyres or being obliged to use the same tyre under a red flag. Meanwhile, Red Bull's advisor Helmut Marko is pushing for Monaco circuit improvements, including possible mandatory pit stops or required use of all three tyre compounds to increase strategy and overtaking opportunities.
